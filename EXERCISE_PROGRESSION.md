## PUSH DAY PROGRESSIONS

### 1. Push-Up Progression
**Foundation (Weeks 1-4)**: Regular Push-Up
- Hands shoulder-width, lower chest to floor

**Intermediate (Weeks 5-8)**: Deficit Push-Up
- Hands on 6" blocks, lower chest BELOW hands
- Deeper range = more chest stretch

**Advanced (Weeks 9+)**: Pseudo-Planche Push-Up
- Hands by ribs, lean forward
- Shoulders past hands = extreme difficulty

### 2. Pike Push-Up Progression
**Foundation**: Pike Push-Up (floor)
- Hips high, lower head to floor

**Intermediate**: Feet Elevated Pike
- Feet on chair, steeper angle = more shoulder load

**Advanced**: Wall Handstand Push-Up
- Feet on wall, full handstand position
- Ultimate shoulder exercise

### 3. Tricep Extension Progression
**Foundation**: TRX Tricep Extension
- Standard angle, controlled tempo

**Intermediate**: Slow Tempo (3-1-1)
- 3 seconds down, 1 second pause, 1 second up

**Advanced**: Feet Closer to Anchor
- Harder angle = more bodyweight load

## LEGS DAY PROGRESSIONS

### 1. Bulgarian Split Squat Progression
**Foundation**: Bodyweight
- Rear foot elevated, controlled tempo

**Intermediate**: Tempo (3-1-1)
- 3 seconds down, 1 second pause, explode up

**Advanced**: Weighted (Backpack)
- Add 10-20 lbs progressively

### 2. Pistol Squat Progression
**Foundation**: TRX Full Assist
- Hold TRX with both hands

**Intermediate**: Fingertip Assist
- Minimal support, building strength

**Advanced**: Full Pistol (No Assist)
- Elite single-leg strength

### 3. Hamstring Curl Progression
**Foundation**: Double Leg
- Both heels in TRX

**Intermediate**: Single Leg
- One heel in TRX, much harder

**Advanced**: Tempo + Pause
- 3 seconds out, 2 second pause, 1 second in

## Progression Timing

### When to Progress
âœ… **Hit 20 reps on all sets** (3-4 sets) for 2 consecutive sessions
âœ… **Exercise feels easy** at current difficulty
âœ… **Form is perfect** (no compensation patterns)

### How to Progress
1. **Increase reps** (6 â†’ 20 range)
2. **Slow tempo** (3-1-1: 3s eccentric, 1s pause, 1s concentric)
3. **Harder angle** (TRX: feet further out, body more horizontal)
4. **Add ROM** (deficit, elevated feet, deeper range)
5. **Add instability** (single limb, pause at bottom)
6. **Add weight** (backpack with books, weighted vest)

### Deload Weeks
Every 4-8 weeks, take a deload:
- Reduce volume by 50% (2 sets instead of 4)
- Same exercises, easier variations
- Recover, prevent overtraining
- Come back stronger

## Evidence-Based Principles

1. **Progressive Overload**: Must increase difficulty over time
2. **Specificity**: Train movement patterns, not isolated muscles
3. **Volume**: 6-8 hard sets per muscle per session
4. **Frequency**: 2x/week per muscle (PPL does this naturally)
5. **Recovery**: 48 hours between same muscle groups
6. **Periodization**: Vary difficulty every 4 weeks
